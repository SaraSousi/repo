    Data Analysis Project

    Investigating the Signature Genes of Atopic Dermatitis and Psoriasis

    I investigated the signature genes of atopic dermatitis and psoriasis from high-dimensional RNA-Seq data with machine learning methods during my Master's at Imperial.


    http://slaviana.co.uk/genes

    Created by:

    Slaviana Pavlovich

    Completed on:

    17th June 2020

    Skills:

    Python / Plotly / MkDocs / HTML5 / CSS3 / JS

    Atopic Dermatitis and Psoriasis are skin conditions affecting approximately 20% and 2% of the global population respectively. Although these diseases are well known to physicians and have been for some time, their causes have eluded scientists up until fairly recently. In this project, we sought to uncover some of the inherent genetic causes of these conditions by identifying key genetic markers that may make people more susceptible to the diseases. To do this, a dataset containing the genes of both healthy and unhealthy patients was scrutinised using a variety of Machine Learning techniques. The results of this study have highlighted a number of genes that may place people at a greater disposition to these conditions.

    Acknowledgment: This project was submitted as part of my MSc in Bioinformatics and Theoretical Systems Biology at Imperial College London.

    Computing Project

    Microbiome Metabolism Exploration Tool

    A personalised human microbiome metabolism exploration tool for clinicians and researchers created during my Master's at Imperial.

    Created by:

    Slaviana Pavlovich, Ariane Duverdier, Ruth Richards and Felix O'Farrell

    Completed on:

    30th March 2020

    Skills:

    Python / MySQL / Flask / HTML5 / CSS3 / JS

    The Microbiome Metabolism Exploration Tool (Memento) is a “1-click” microbiome analysis web tool for clinicians. Memento receives a person of interest’s (POI’s) raw 16S amplicon sequence information and infers the significant enzymatic and metabolic pathways taking place. After querying the Memento database (consisting of 2062 microbiome associated enzymatic reactions and 1176 exogenous compounds), the tool outputs the likely interactions of a given drug in the POI’s microbiome. As a tool aimed at clinicians and researchers, Memento associates 144 academic publications and 1185 applications of drugs to the database to allow the clinician to browse relevant texts alongside the tool. Memento also tailors a premade Operational Taxonomic Unit (OTU) taxonomic classifier (from scikit-learn) to give researchers and clinicians a high-level view of dysbiosis in the POI’s microbiome (with up to 85% accuracy). Memento was validated using data from recent 16S rRNA amplicon studies and reproduces similar trends to the current consensus, whilst also providing clinically relevant drug and publication information.

    As a proof-of-concept, Memento has demonstrated that highly relevant clinical information can be extracted from 16S amplicon data. The web tool serves as a benchmark for new user-friendly bioinformatic implementations aimed to take steps towards personalised medicine in the microbiome clinical setting.

    Acknowledgment: This project was submitted as part of my MSc in Bioinformatics and Theoretical Systems Biology at Imperial College London.

    My Dissertation

    To what extent can the analysis of online hotel reviews improve the understanding of customer satisfaction in the hotel industry?

    This dissertation examines the extent to which the analysis of online hotel reviews can improve the understanding of customer satisfaction in the hotel industry.

    Created by:

    Slaviana Pavlovich

    Completed on:

    15th April 2019

    Skills:

    Python / R / Natural Language Processing

    An innovative research method, data mining, was employed for data cleaning, pre-processing and analysis of 515,743 customer hotel reviews from Booking.com. The analysis offers new insights into the key determinants of customer satisfaction, showing that customers particularly value the locations of hotels, friendly staff and clean rooms. There is a moderate positive correlation between customer sentiment (emotion expressed in the text of the review) and given rating. To address an inconsistency that could occur between customer sentiments and ratings, with the latter being potentially misleading and not supported by the text of the review, the supervised machine learning techniques were compared on the ability to predict the customer ratings, on the 10-star scale, based on reviewers’ text. Support Vector Machines algorithm delivers the most accurate results, achieving an accuracy of 76%. The findings from this research suggest that the analysis of online reviews can provide the means for bridging the gap between customers’ perception of service quality and managers’ understanding of it.

    Acknowledgment: This dissertation was submitted as part of my BSc in Information Management for Business at University College London.
